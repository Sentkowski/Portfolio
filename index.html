About me

I spent years writing prose. From creepypastas to short stories which were acknowledged in literary contests. But it was the source code that made me feel that the letters I write are somehow powerful.

As a child I encountered variables for the first time in Warcraft's III Map Editor. The next time was in 2018 when I decided to give Python a try on CodeAcademy.

Just a few weeks later I created my first piece of software. It computed Nash equilibrium. It looked awful... but you can check.

Later - thanks to Harvard's CS50 online courses - I dived deeper into web development.

Right now I am soon to start the last year of practical linguistics and copywriting at Nicolaus Copernicus University in Toruń.

Even if it's not really about coding, I take it as an opportunity to explore topics related to the web: UX/UI, design and electronic literature.

I believe that this is the right time for me to take the next step on this path – to enter the industry. I would be delighted if you could support me in doing that.
