About me

I spent years writing prose. From creepypastas to short stories which were acknowledged in literary contests. But it was the source code that made me feel that the letters I write have the power to make a change – even if a tiny one – in the world.

I encountered variables for the first time when I was no more than 12 years old, in Warcraft's III Map Editor. Since then this obscure concept lied untouched in my head, until one day in 2018 I decided to give Python a try on CodeAcademy.

Just a few weeks later I created my first piece of software that actually did something useful: computed Nash equilibrium. And of course it looked awful... but you can have a look.

Diving deeper into the world of computer science - thanks to Harvard's CS50 online courses - I discovered web development. Every next step was like a tiny revelation, unveiling new possibilities.

Right now I am soon to start the last year of studying practical linguistics and copywriting. Even if it doesn't have much to do with coding, I take it as an opportunity to explore topics related to the web, such as UX/UI, design and electronic literature.

I believe that this is the right time for me to take the next step on this path – to enter the industry. I would be delgihted if you could support me in doing that.
